Let's face it. Weather is hard to implement in Minecraft and everyone wants it done a different way. Large technical changes, rich seasonal mechanics, and a frequent game drops can become a burden to a modder. Some vegetation look off from the current biome. 

Real Seasons focuses on keeping it simple, keeping it as immersive as possible, keeping it not disruptive to your experience, and keeping it available for the latest version.

In this mod, color is the forefront for immersion. It attempts to make plants make sense in where and when they are. Just like in the real world, plants don't care when any season officially start. The colors will all change gradually, and most of the time the season's peak colors will not show up exactly on each quarter.

The world is changing without letting game mechanics take the lead. Just some colors and imagination can go a long way.

Seasons, the way it should feel.

# Features

## Purely Aesthetic

Real Seasons only changes how the world looks.

* No gameplay changes
* No crop mechanics
* No temperature systems
* No weather modifications
* No snow accumulation changes
* No new blocks

Your Minecraft experience remains completely vanilla. The world simply looks different as the year progresses.

## Client Side Only

Real Seasons runs entirely on the client.

* Works in singleplayer
* Works on multiplayer servers
* Does not require installation on the server
* Will never crash because a server does not have the mod installed

Simply install the mod and enjoy seasonal visuals anywhere.

## Seasons That Feel Natural

Real Seasons does not treat the year as four identical blocks.

The visual peak of each season is intentionally offset to create a more natural rhythm throughout the year.

* Spring reaches its most vibrant greens around mid-spring
* Summer gradually becomes drier and less saturated
* Fall colors arrive later and transition quickly
* Winter starts cold and barren before slowly becoming greener again

... and that's just for temperate biomes.

This creates seasonal transitions that feel more organic and believable than simple quarter-year color swaps.

## Biome-Specific Seasonal Colors

Every biome is individually tailored to make sense throughout the year.

A lush forest should not transition the same way a savanna does. Swamps should not behave like snowy plains.

Real Seasons gives each biome its own seasonal personality, creating a more natural and believable world.

## Every Tree Matters

Seasonal colors are not limited to deciduous trees.

Every tree type has been carefully considered so that it feels at home in both its biome and the current season. Vegetation does not simply keep its vanilla appearance because it happens to be evergreen, nor does every tree change in the same way.

The same species can look different depending on where it grows. Trees in dry, arid climates should not have the same richness as those in cool, temperate forests. Likewise, vegetation in wetlands, mountains, and tropical regions each follows its own seasonal character.

Rather than applying a universal color shift across the world, Real Seasons gives every tree the opportunity to reflect both its environment and the time of year, resulting in landscapes that feel more natural and cohesive wherever you explore.


## Real Time Support

Want your Minecraft world to match the real world?

Real Seasons can synchronize with your local calendar so that seasons progress at the same pace as they do outside your window.

When spring arrives in real life, spring arrives in Minecraft.

## Flexible In-Game Seasons

Prefer seasons tied to Minecraft time instead?

Real Seasons supports in-game seasons. Sub seasons included!

* 1 to 100 sub-seasons allowed!
* Each sub-season must be at least 5 in-game days long
* Smooth transitions between all seasonal stages. For best results, more than 3 sub-seasons is recommended. 

Create simple four-season cycles or detailed year-long progressions with as many transitional periods as you want.

## Easy Configuration

All settings can be edited through Mod Menu.

Adjust season lengths, time systems, and other options without manually editing configuration files.

## Artistic Interpretation of Seasons

Real Seasons is not designed around preserving vanilla's colors.

The colors used throughout the year are an intentional interpretation of how each season should feel, rather than a direct modification of Minecraft's default palette.

As a result, vanilla's grass and foliage colors may not correspond to any particular point in the seasonal cycle. Some vanilla colors may appear briefly, while others may never appear at all.

The goal is not to make the world look like vanilla Minecraft with seasonal tints applied. The goal is to make each season evoke the colors players naturally associate with that time of year.

Spring should feel fresh.

Summer should feel warm and sun-soaked.

Autumn should feel vibrant and fleeting.

Winter should feel quiet, dormant, and worn.

Every color throughout the year is chosen with that philosophy in mind.

# Compatibility

## Supported

* Minecraft's latest supported version
* Multiplayer servers
* Singleplayer worlds
* Voxy
* Distant Horizons

## Currently Unsupported

* Modded biome color integration. Currently looking into datapack solutions.
* Dynamically changing blocks that are not affected by color maps. This is planned if there is a way.

## Permanently Unsupported Features

* Sub-seasons less than 5 days to minimize reload disruption
* Weather changes. This will never be a feature unless if Minecraft overhauls their weather engine.
* Additional modded blocks or items. You look outside to see what season it is.

# Project Philosophy

Real Seasons is designed around one core principle:

**Always stay modern.**

The priority is keeping the mod updated to the latest Minecraft versions as quickly as possible. Compatibility with older versions and secondary integrations will always come after maintaining support for modern Minecraft.

The goal is simple:

Beautiful, realistic seasons that feel natural, stay up to date, and never interfere with gameplay.
